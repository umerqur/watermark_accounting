# Watermark Taxes - Logo Concepts

## Overview

Four modern corporate logo concepts for Watermark Taxes, designed to replace the current shield icon with a contemporary, professional brand identity.

## Design Principles

- **Modern wordmark style** with strong sans-serif typography (Inter font)
- **Clean horizontal layout** with icon on the left, text on the right
- **Blue color palette** maintaining brand consistency
- **Premium corporate aesthetic** avoiding clipart styling
- **Scalable and versatile** for all applications

## Logo Concepts

### Concept 1: Wave
**Theme:** Flowing watermark representation

**Design:**
- Two flowing wave lines representing the "watermark" concept
- Symbolizes transparency, clarity, and fluidity
- Colors: #0052A3 (medium blue) and #003D6B (primary blue)

**Best for:** Emphasizing the "Watermark" brand name with visual storytelling

**Files:**
- `logo-final-wave.svg` - Complete logo
- Preview in `logo-preview.html`

### Concept 2: Data Bars
**Theme:** Financial growth and data analysis

**Design:**
- Four ascending bars representing growth and progress
- Symbolizes data-driven approach and financial improvement
- Gradient of blues: #003D6B → #0052A3 → #0066CC → #00A9A5

**Best for:** Conveying analytical expertise and progressive service

**Files:**
- `logo-final-bars.svg` - Complete logo
- Preview in `logo-preview.html`

---

### Concept 3: Verified
**Theme:** Trust and accuracy

**Design:**
- Bold checkmark within a circular frame
- Represents verification, accuracy, and trustworthiness
- Circle: #003D6B (primary blue), Check: #00A9A5 (teal accent)

**Best for:** Emphasizing reliability and certified professional services

**Files:**
- `logo-final-checkmark.svg` - Complete logo
- Preview in `logo-preview.html`

### Concept 4: WT Monogram
**Theme:** Sophisticated lettermark

**Design:**
- Clean monogram combining W and T letters
- Geometric square container with rounded corners
- Background: #003D6B, W: white, T: #00A9A5

**Best for:** Creating a memorable, premium brand mark that scales well
